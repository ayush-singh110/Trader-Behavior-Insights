# 📊 Trading Analytics Report

## 🪙 Key Market & Coin Insights

- **HYPE** is the most actively traded coin across the dataset
- **BTC** and **@142** consistently show high execution prices, indicating higher-value trades
- **@142** stands out as the coin with the highest execution price specifically in Simple Sell trades
- During **Fear** sentiment, HYPE remains the most traded coin with immediate execution, suggesting strong liquidity and trader confidence even in fearful conditions

---

## 💰 Profit & Loss (PnL) Behavior

- Early trading periods were relatively stable, with minimal profit and loss fluctuations
- In later periods, **PnL volatility increased significantly**, marked by sharp gains and losses
- While both Fear and Greed sentiments exhibit volatility:
  - **Greed periods** show sharper spikes, reflecting aggressive risk-taking
  - **Fear periods** show more uneven and abrupt swings, indicating higher uncertainty in trader outcomes

---

## 📈 Trade Value Trends

- Trade value has increased sharply over time, especially after 2024
- In 2025, trade values became significantly more volatile, reflecting heightened market activity
- Across all sentiment categories:
  - **Fear and Greed periods** exhibit the largest spikes in trade value, indicating intense trading activity during emotionally charged market conditions

## 💸 Fees & Trading Costs

- Average trading fees increase over time
- Fees become more volatile in later periods, aligning with:
  - Higher trade values
  - Increased trading frequency
  - Larger position sizes

---

## 📉 Distribution & Correlation Analysis

- The data shows a clear presence of **outliers**, especially in:
  - Trade value
  - Closed PnL
  
  These represent rare but high-impact trades, common in leveraged markets

- The correlation heatmap reveals:
  - A **strong positive correlation** between trade size (USD) and fees
  - Most other numerical features show weak or no direct correlation, suggesting independent trading behaviors

---

## 🎯 Overall Takeaway

Market sentiment significantly influences trading intensity, risk-taking behavior, and volatility. **Fear and Greed periods** drive larger trades, higher fees, and extreme outcomes.
